Lahmacun

Description

Lahmacun in Arabic means "meat with dough" and it is a popular recipe in Turkish cuisine that looks similar to a pizza. A thin dough, which is given a circular shape and covered with a mixture of minced meat, onion, parsley, bell peppers and spices; to later cook in the oven.

We teach you how to make the traditional Turkish pizza, from the dough!

Ingredients

For the dough

  • 350 g flour
  • 200 ml water
  • 20 g fresh yeast (or 6 g dry baker's yeast)
  • 2 tbs olive oil
  • Warm water

For the filling

  • 250 g minced meat (lamb or beef)
  • 1 tomato
  • 1 green pepper
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 onion
  • bell pepper sauce
  • parsley
  • ground black pepper
  • salt
  • olive oil
  • 1 chilli (optional)

Steps

For the dough

  1. In a bowl, mix the sifted flour with salt.
  2. Make a hole in the middle and add the crumbled yeast and the lukewarm water.
  3. Add the olive oil and the lukewarm water, and mix.
  4. Knead the mixture with your hands on a floured surface for a few minutes, adding a little flour until it is an elastic dough without lumps and it comes away from your hands.
  5. Let the ball of dough rest in a bowl covered with plastic wrap in a warm place for 30 minutes, until doubled in volume.
  6. After that time, knead again and divide the dough into equal portions.
  7. Stretch with the roller and flatten the dough, giving it the shape of circles.

For the filling

  1. Chop the garlic, the onion, the tomato and the chilli and mix with the minced meat.
  2. Add the parsley, the bell pepper sauce, salt and pepper. Mix with a spoon.

Now it's time to send that lahmacun to the oven!

  1. Preheat the oven at 275ÂºC.
  2. Place the pizza bases on a tray lined with parchment paper.
  3. Cover the surface of each one of the bases with the filling.
  4. Put the lahmacun in the oven for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Serve. You can add a final touch by adding a squeeze of lemon juice and a few slices of red onion, chopped tomato and parsley.
