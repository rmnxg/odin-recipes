Lasagna

Description

Today I'm going to teach you how to make an authentic lasagna as if I were an Italian nonna from the 20th century. That is to say, without skimping on time, love or quality of the ingredients.

I invite you to see how it's done and prepare it yourselves. Give it a shot!

Ingredients

For the filling

  • 600 g beef
  • 300 g bacon
  • 1 onion
  • 2 carrots
  • 1 celery branch
  • 800 g peeled tomatoes, or tomato sauce
  • 50 ml milk
  • 3 tbs olive oil
  • salt (to taste)
  • ground black pepper (to taste)

For the dough

  • 200 g common flour
  • 2 big eggs
  • 1-2 tbs olive oil
  • 1 pinch of salt

For the bechamel

  • 80 g butter
  • 80 g common flour
  • 750 ml milk (cold)
  • nutmeg to taste
  • salt to taste
  • black pepper to taste
  • 200 g grated parmesan

Steps

For the filling

  1. Cut the bacon, the onion, the carrots and the celery branch into small squares.
  2. Put the bacon in a pan over medium heat. Don't forget to stir it from time to time!
  3. Add a splash of oil, the cut vegetables and a pich of salt to the pan.
  4. Change to medium low heat and cook until the onion, the carrot and the celery soften.
  5. Add the beef. Stir until it changes color.
  6. Add the chopped tomatoes or the tomato sauce.
  7. Once the sauce begins to boil, change the heat level to low. Then place the lid over the pan, but leave a gap for the steam to escape.
  8. Let cook for 2-4 hours (if you notice that the sauce is getting dry, add some more water or broth).
  9. Season to taste.
  10. Add the milk to balance the acidity.

For the dough

  1. Sift the flour into a bowl.
  2. Add a pinch of salt, the eggs, and a splash of oil. Stir very well.
  3. Finish making the dough ball with your hand. Keep in mind this dough shouldn't be kneaded much. Just integrate all its ingredients well.
  4. Cover and leave to rest for 20 minutes.
  5. Stretch the dough with a rolling pin. The ideal dough thickness is when it becomes a bit transparent.
  6. Cut the dough in sheets size 10x12 cm. Approximately 17 sheets will come out.
  7. Cook the dough in plenty of salted water for about 1 minute. Ideally in 2 batches so that the sheets don't stick together.
  8. Rinse with cold water and place a cloth on it to dry.

For the bechamel

  1. Melt the butter.
  2. Add the flour. Sauté for 2-3 minutes over medium heat.
  3. Add the cold milk and switch to high heat (it is important that the milk is cold because flour dissolves very well in it).
  4. Stir until thick.
  5. As soon as it starts to boil, switch to medium heat and cook it for 2-3 more minutes.
  6. Once removed from the heat, add salt, ground black pepper and nutmeg to taste.
  7. Cover it with plastic wrap or similar to prevent the formation of a crust.

Now that you have your filling, your dough and your bechamel ready, it's time to assemble your lasagna!

  1. Preheat the oven to 180ºc with heat from above and below.
  2. Grease the mold with the butter.
  3. Cover the bottom with a layer of bechamel.
  4. Now repeat this layer process until the mold is filled:
    1. dough
    2. filling
    3. bechamel
    4. Parmesan
  5. Generously cover the last layer with bechamel and sprinkle with Parmesan.
  6. Bake for 45 minutes, the last 5 on the grill at 250ºc.
  7. Let it rest for 10-15 minutes before serving.
